# 06 - Data Warehousing

## What This Section Covers

This section covers data warehouse concepts, warehouse layers, star schemas, facts, dimensions, marts, slowly changing dimensions, metrics, and analytics-ready data design.

## Why It Matters In Data Engineering

Warehouses turn raw operational data into trusted datasets for reporting, analytics, finance, planning, and leadership decisions. A well-designed warehouse reduces confusion and repeated manual analysis.

## What You Should Be Able To Do

After studying this section, you should be able to:

- Explain the purpose of a data warehouse.
- Design fact and dimension tables.
- Separate raw, staging, intermediate, and mart layers.
- Define reusable business metrics.
- Build datasets that are easy for analysts and BI tools to use.
- Validate warehouse tables with data quality checks.
